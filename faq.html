About Us

About Vegora Bites

Vegora Bites is a free plant-based recipe platform and animal welfare advocacy site. We provide delicious, easy-to-follow vegan recipes, educational content about animal cruelty, and resources to help people transition to a more compassionate lifestyle. Founded in 2020, we've grown into a global community of plant-based food lovers.

Yes! All of our recipes, articles, tools (like the meal planner and 30-day challenge), and educational content are completely free. We believe access to healthy, compassionate food information should be available to everyone. We're supported through partnerships and community donations.

The best ways to support us are: sharing our recipes and content with friends and family, subscribing to our newsletter, taking the plant-based pledge, participating in the 30-day challenge, and spreading the word on social media. Every share helps us reach more people and make a bigger impact for animals.

Going Vegan

Plant-Based Living

Of course! Every plant-based meal makes a difference. You don't have to be "all or nothing." Try Meatless Mondays, swap dairy milk for oat milk, or just explore one new vegan recipe per week. Our pledge system lets you choose your own commitment level -- from one meal a day to fully plant-based. Progress, not perfection!

A well-planned vegan diet provides all essential nutrients. The key supplements to consider are Vitamin B12 (essential -- take a supplement or eat fortified foods daily), Vitamin D (especially in winter), and Omega-3s (from flaxseed, chia seeds, or algae-based supplements). Eat a variety of whole foods and you'll meet your protein, iron, calcium, and zinc needs naturally.

Eating out as a vegan is easier than ever! Most restaurants now offer plant-based options. Asian (Thai, Indian, Chinese, Japanese), Mediterranean, Mexican, and Ethiopian cuisines have naturally vegan dishes. Apps like HappyCow help you find vegan-friendly restaurants. Don't be afraid to ask your server about modifications -- most kitchens are happy to accommodate.

Be confident but not pushy. Offer to bring a dish to gatherings (it's a great way to introduce people to vegan food). Communicate your dietary needs ahead of time for events. Focus on sharing delicious food rather than debating ethics at the dinner table. Most people are curious and supportive once they try great vegan food. Lead by example!
