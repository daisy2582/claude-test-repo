💛 Rescued

Animal Stories

Meet the animals whose lives were saved. Each one has a name, a personality, and a story of hope.

🐄

Bella the Cow

Rescued from a dairy farm in 2021

Bella was just days away from being sent to slaughter when she was rescued from a closing dairy farm. Malnourished and frightened, she spent her first weeks at the sanctuary hiding in corners. Today, Bella is a gentle giant who loves head scratches and has become the unofficial greeter for all new arrivals. She spends her days grazing in open fields and napping in the sunshine.

Living happily at Green Acres Sanctuary
🐷

Oliver the Pig

Fell off a transport truck in 2022

Oliver literally fell off a truck on its way to a slaughterhouse. Found on the roadside by a passing driver, he was brought to an emergency vet with broken ribs and severe bruising. After months of recovery, Oliver revealed his playful personality. He loves belly rubs, playing in mud puddles, and following volunteers around like a puppy.

Thriving at Heartland Animal Sanctuary
🐔

Rosie the Hen

Rescued from a battery cage farm in 2023

Rosie had never seen sunlight, felt grass, or spread her wings for the first two years of her life. When she was rescued from a shuttered egg farm, she was nearly featherless and terrified of open spaces. Slowly, with patience and care, Rosie learned to trust again. She now has a full coat of beautiful red feathers and loves dust bathing in the garden.

Free-ranging at Sunrise Sanctuary
🐑

Woolly the Sheep

Found abandoned in a field in 2022

Woolly was found alone in an abandoned field, his wool so overgrown he could barely walk. Carrying over 35 pounds of excess wool, he was suffering from skin infections and heat exhaustion. After his rescue shearing, Woolly emerged as a playful, curious sheep who loves to run and play with the sanctuary dogs.

Living his best life at Mountain View Farm
🐇

Clover the Rabbit

Rescued from a testing lab in 2023

Clover spent the first year of her life in a small cage at a cosmetics testing facility. She had never hopped freely or felt soft bedding. When the facility was shut down, Clover was among 200 rabbits rehomed. At first she would freeze when anyone approached, but now she binkies (jumps for joy) every morning and loves fresh herbs from the garden.

Hopping freely at Bunny Haven Rescue
🐟

Finn the Fish

Rescued from a draining pond in 2024

When a local ornamental pond was drained for construction, Finn and dozens of other koi were going to be discarded. A volunteer team rescued them and brought them to a wildlife sanctuary. Finn, a magnificent golden koi, now lives in a spacious natural pond where he can swim freely. He's become an ambassador for fish welfare awareness.

Swimming peacefully at Clearwater Sanctuary
