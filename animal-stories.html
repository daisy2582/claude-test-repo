Stories of Hope

Rescued Animal
Stories

Behind every rescue is a story of resilience, compassion, and a second chance at life. Meet some of the animals who found safety and love through our sanctuary partners.

Meet the Rescues

Their Stories, Their Lives

Each name represents a life saved and a bond formed

🐄

Bella

Holstein Dairy Cow | Rescued 2021

Bella was rescued from a dairy farm where she had been impregnated seven times. When her milk production declined, she was scheduled for slaughter. Our partner sanctuary intercepted her transport, and today Bella grazes freely on 20 acres of pasture. She loves belly rubs and has formed an inseparable bond with a rescued calf named Clover.

Today: Bella lives at Green Meadow Sanctuary with her best friend Clover. She has not been separated from a calf since.
🐷

Oliver

Yorkshire Pig | Rescued 2022

Oliver fell off a transport truck on his way to a slaughterhouse. Found injured on the side of the road, a passerby called our rescue hotline. After months of veterinary care for his broken leg and respiratory infection from factory farm conditions, Oliver made a full recovery. He is now one of the most playful residents at the sanctuary.

Today: Oliver loves mud baths, belly scratches, and playing with his toy ball. He greets every visitor with enthusiastic oinks.
🐔

Rosie

Leghorn Hen | Rescued 2023

Rosie was one of 50 hens rescued from a battery cage facility that was being shut down. She had never seen sunlight, walked on grass, or spread her wings. Her beak had been cut short and her feathers were nearly gone from stress. In the first weeks at the sanctuary, she was terrified of open spaces.

Today: Rosie has grown back her beautiful white feathers and spends her days dust-bathing in the sun. She lays eggs when she chooses — and eats them herself for nutrition.
🐑

Winston

Merino Sheep | Rescued 2022

Winston was found wandering near a wool processing facility, severely overgrown and blinded by his own wool. He had been discarded when he became too old for profitable shearing. His wool weighed nearly 30 pounds — a painful burden caused by selective breeding. The rescue team carefully sheared him over two days.

Today: Winston can finally see! He follows his caretakers around the sanctuary and has become best friends with a rescue goat named Pepper.
🐇

Luna

New Zealand White Rabbit | Rescued 2023

Luna was rescued from a cosmetics testing laboratory when it was closed following an animal cruelty investigation. She had spent her entire life in a small wire cage, subjected to skin irritation tests. Her eyes were scarred from chemical exposure. When she first arrived at the sanctuary, she was afraid of being touched.

Today: Luna hops freely in a spacious garden enclosure with three other rescued rabbits. She now enjoys being gently petted and has discovered her love for fresh dandelion greens.
🦃

Theodore

Broad-Breasted White Turkey | Rescued 2023

Theodore was rescued just days before Thanksgiving from a factory farm. Like most commercial turkeys, he had been bred to grow so fast that his legs could barely support his weight. He had never been outdoors or felt grass under his feet. His first steps outside were wobbly but determined.

Today: With a specialized diet and physical therapy, Theodore can walk comfortably. He loves showing off his feathers to visitors and gobbles enthusiastically at feeding time.
🐐

Pepper

Pygmy Goat | Rescued 2021

Pepper was found abandoned in a field after a petting zoo closed down. She was severely malnourished and suffering from untreated parasites. Her hooves had grown so long from neglect that she could barely walk. The veterinary team at the sanctuary spent weeks nursing her back to health.

Today: Pepper is the most mischievous resident at the sanctuary! She loves climbing on everything, stealing hats from visitors, and napping in sunbeams with her best friend Winston.
🐄

Clover

Jersey Calf | Rescued 2022

Clover was just three days old when she was taken from her mother at a dairy farm and sold to a veal operation. A compassionate worker at the facility contacted our rescue network, and we were able to negotiate her release. She arrived at the sanctuary small, scared, and crying for her mother.

Today: Though Clover could not be reunited with her birth mother, she found a surrogate in Bella. The two are inseparable — they eat together, sleep together, and groom each other every day.
